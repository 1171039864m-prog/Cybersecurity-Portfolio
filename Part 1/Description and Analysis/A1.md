I identified a CCTV (Closed-Circuit Television) surveillance camera mounted at a low-traffic area at the University of Western Australia. This is a Physical Detective Control. While its presence acts as a deterrent, its primary function in security is to detect and record detect suspicious events in low-traffic area and unauthorized physical access to campus infrastructure.

By monitoring restricted areas (like server rooms or network hubs), CCTV protects the Availability of the network. If an intruder physically tampers with a switch or pulls a cable, the footage provides the necessary evidence to identify the cause of the downtime.

Surveillance logs act as "Physical Event Logs." Just as a firewall logs a digital intrusion, these cameras log physical intrusions. This is critical for Non-repudiation—providing undeniable proof of who was in a specific location at a specific time.
