I identified the use of Third-Party Cookie Blocking and Incognito/Private Browsing in the Google Chrome browser. Cookies are small files used by websites to remember stateful information, such as login status, shopping cart contents, or user preferences. However, "third-party" cookies are often used by advertisers to track a user's behavior across multiple different websites without their explicit consent. By enabling the "Block third-party cookies" setting, I am preventing cross-site tracking. This reduces the amount of personal data (browsing habits) collected by data brokers and advertising networks, thereby enhancing my online anonymity.
