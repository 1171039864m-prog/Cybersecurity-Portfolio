1.Optus Data Breach (Sep 2022)

In September 2022, Optus suffered a major data breach affecting up to 10 million customers, where personal information such as names, addresses, and identification numbers was exposed. The breach was linked to a vulnerability in an exposed API, allowing attackers to access sensitive data.

  This incident highlights the importance of secure API design and proper access control to prevent large-scale data exposure and identity theft risks.


2. Medibank Data Breach (Oct-Nov 2022)

In October–November 2022, Medibank was targeted by a ransomware attack where attackers accessed and exfiltrated sensitive customer data. When ransom demands were not met, the data was published online, exposing highly sensitive medical information.

  This incident demonstrates the serious consequences of data breaches involving health data and the need for robust cybersecurity measures.
  

3. Latitude Financial Data Breach (March 2023)

In March 2023, Latitude Financial Services suffered a cyberattack in which attackers gained unauthorised access to customer data through compromised employee credentials. The breach exposed millions of records, including identificationdocuments such as driver’s licences and passports.
This incident highlights the risks of credential compromise and demonstrates the importance of strong authentication and access control to protect sensitive financial information.
