1.  Ingram Micro Ransomware Attack (July 2025)

In July 2025, Ingram Micro experienced a ransomware attack in which an unauthorised third party accessed internal systems and exfiltrated sensitive data. The breach occurred between July 2 and 3, during which attackers obtained employee and applicant information, including identification details.

  The attack disrupted operations and highlighted the impact of ransomware on global supply chains. It demonstrates the importance of strong system security, monitoring, and incident response to minimise damage from cyberattacks.

2. U.S. Treasury Hack (Dec 2024)

In December 2024, the United States Department of the Treasury was compromised in a cyberattack attributed to a state-sponsored Chinese APT group. Attackers exploited a third-party vendor, BeyondTrust, using a stolen API key to gain unauthorised access to Treasury workstations and unclassified documents.

  This incident highlights the risks of supply-chain attacks and third-party dependencies, demonstrating the need for strict access control and secure integration with external vendors.

3. MOVEit Transfer Data Breach (May-June 2023)

In May–June 2023, a critical zero-day vulnerability in the MOVEit Transfer file transfer software was exploited by attackers to gain unauthorised access to sensitive data from numerous organisations worldwide. The attack was linked to the Cl0p ransomware group, which used the vulnerability to extract large volumes of data without immediately encrypting systems.

  This incident highlights the risks associated with unpatched software vulnerabilities and demonstrates the importance of timely security updates, vulnerability management, and secure handling of sensitive data.


4.  Snowflake Data Breach Campaign (May-June 2024)

In May–June 2024, multiple organisations were affected by a data breach campaign targeting Snowflake accounts. Attackers used stolen credentials to access customer data stored on the platform, impacting companies such as Ticketmaster and Santander.

  This incident highlights the risks of credential theft and demonstrates the importance of multi-factor authentication and proper account security in cloud environments.


5.  MGM Resorts Cyberattack (Sep 2023)

In September 2023, MGM Resorts experienced a major cyberattack where attackers used social engineering to gain access to internal systems. The breach caused widespread disruption, including shutdown of hotel systems, casinos, and digital services across multiple locations.

  This incident highlights the risks of social engineering attacks and demonstrates the importance of strong identity verification and employee security awareness to prevent unauthorised access.
